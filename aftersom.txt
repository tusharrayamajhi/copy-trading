Once you have initialized the **v2** bank accounts and updated your `constants.ts`, you need to "Fill the Tank" so traders can swap.

### How to Fund the Bank (Adding Liquidity):

The Bank accounts are **Token Accounts**, so you must send SPL Tokens to them.

#### 1. Funding with USDC
1.  **Copy the USDC Bank Address**: Get the `PLATFORM_BANK_USDC` address from your dashboard or script.
2.  **Send from Wallet**: Open your Phantom/Solflare wallet and send your **20 USDC** directly to that bank address.

#### 2. Funding with SOL (MUST be Wrapped SOL)
This is the most important part: Because the Bank is a Token Account, it **cannot** accept Native SOL. It only accepts **Wrapped SOL (WSOL)**.
1.  **Wrap your SOL**: Go to the **Investor Dashboard** on your website and use the "Native SOL" section to wrap some SOL (e.g., 1 SOL).
2.  **Copy the SOL Bank Address**: Get the `PLATFORM_BANK_SOL` address.
3.  **Send WSOL**: In your wallet, find your "Wrapped SOL" and send it to that bank address.

### Why is this necessary?
The platform acts like a mini-DEX. When a trader wants to swap USDC for SOL:
*   They send USDC to the **USDC Bank**.
*   The **SOL Bank** sends WSOL back to the trader.
If the SOL Bank is empty (0.00), the swap will fail with a **"Vault is empty"** error.

**Once you send those tokens, the "Platform Liquidity" card on your website will update, and your traders can start swapping!**

Since you are using a **custom USDC mint** (`Gh9Zw...`) and the SOL bank requires **Wrapped SOL**, you can use this script in **Solana Playground** to fund the bank directly from your wallet.

### Funding Script (Run in Playground):

```typescript
import * as anchor from "@coral-xyz/anchor";
import { 
  getAssociatedTokenAddressSync, 
  createSyncNativeInstruction,
  createTransferInstruction,
  getOrCreateAssociatedTokenAccount
} from "@solana/spl-token";

async function main() {
  const connection = pg.connection;
  const wallet = pg.wallet;

  // 1. BANK ADDRESSES (Copy these from your constants.ts)
  const BANK_SOL = new anchor.web3.PublicKey("YOUR_V2_SOL_BANK_ADDRESS");
  const BANK_USDC = new anchor.web3.PublicKey("YOUR_V2_USDC_BANK_ADDRESS");

  // 2. MINTS
  const WSOL_MINT = new anchor.web3.PublicKey("So11111111111111111111111111111111111111112");
  const USDC_MINT = new anchor.web3.PublicKey("Gh9ZwEmdLJ8DscKNTkTqPbNwLNNBjuSzaG9Vp2KGtKJr");

  const tx = new anchor.web3.Transaction();

  // --- PART A: Send 1.0 Wrapped SOL to Bank ---
  console.log("Preparing 1.0 SOL Liquidity...");
  const userWsolAta = getAssociatedTokenAddressSync(WSOL_MINT, wallet.publicKey);
  
  // Transfer 1 SOL to your WSOL ATA and sync
  tx.add(anchor.web3.SystemProgram.transfer({
    fromPubkey: wallet.publicKey,
    toPubkey: userWsolAta,
    lamports: 1 * anchor.web3.LAMPORTS_PER_SOL,
  }));
  tx.add(createSyncNativeInstruction(userWsolAta));

  // Send the WSOL to the SOL Bank
  tx.add(createTransferInstruction(userWsolAta, BANK_SOL, wallet.publicKey, 1 * anchor.web3.LAMPORTS_PER_SOL));

  // --- PART B: Send USDC to Bank ---
  console.log("Preparing USDC Liquidity...");
  const userUsdcAta = getAssociatedTokenAddressSync(USDC_MINT, wallet.publicKey);
  
  // Send 50 USDC (assuming you have it in your wallet)
  // USDC in Playground usually has 6 decimals
  tx.add(createTransferInstruction(userUsdcAta, BANK_USDC, wallet.publicKey, 50 * 1000000));

  console.log("Sending liquidity to Bank...");
  const sig = await pg.program.provider.sendAndConfirm(tx);
  console.log("✅ Liquidity added! Signature:", sig);
}

main();
```

### Tips for Faucets:
1.  **For SOL**: Use any standard Devnet faucet (like `solana airdrop 2`) to get native SOL.
2.  **For USDC**: If you are using the Playground USDC (`Gh9Zw...`), you can mint more tokens to yourself in the Playground **"Mints"** tab (click the `+` icon next to the mint).
3.  **For WSOL**: The script above handles "Wrapping" the SOL for you by converting your native SOL into tokens before sending.

Once you run this, your **Navbar** and **Bank Status** will finally show the liquidity!
